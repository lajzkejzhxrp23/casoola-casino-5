How the peg pyramid works

The board is built as a triangle of staggered pegs, with each lower row one peg wider than the row above it. A ball dropped at the top has a roughly even chance of moving left or right at each peg, and that pattern repeats until it reaches the bottom.

Because every step is a split in two directions, most balls collect near the middle pockets while the edges are hit less often. Mathematicians describe the layout as a Galton board, and the payout table follows the same curve in reverse: common middle pockets pay less, while the rare edge pockets pay more.

The peg lines a ball crosses; more rows mean more pockets and a steeper odds curve.
